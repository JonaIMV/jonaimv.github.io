The Mexican Constitution allows foreigners to acquire property on the coast (Restricted Zone) through a Bank Trust.This instrument grants you all property rights: you can use, rent, modify, sell, or bequeath the property to whomever you choose, with the bank acting solely as the title administrator.

Sources: Art. 27 of the Constitution & Foreign Investment Law (Art. 10-14).

Foreign banks do not usually mortgage properties in Mexico directly. However, there are excellent alternatives: many developers offer direct financing (without credit checks) during the construction phase (12 to 36 months). In addition, we work with specialized financial institutions (SOFOMES) that grant mortgage loans in pesos or dollars specifically to foreigners.

Sources: Mexican Banking Association (ABM) & Real Estate Market Practices. Real Estate Market Practices.

Income tax (ISR) is paid only on the profit obtained capital gains. If you are a tax resident in Mexico, you can exempt this tax if it is your primary residence. As a foreigner or investor, you can deduct expenses such as real estate commissions, improvements to the property and closing costs to legally reduce the taxable base.

Sources: Law of Income Tax (LISR - Art. 93) & SAT.

You should budget between 5% and 8% of the property's value. This amount covers: Fees of the Public Notary, Property Acquisition Tax (ISAI/ISABI), Public Registration Rights of the Property and the opening fee of the Trust. It is a one-time payment at the time of deed.

In Mexico, a Public Notary is an experienced lawyer designated by the government with public faith. Their role is neutral and essential: they are legally responsible for verifying the chain of titles, ensuring there are no liens, calculating and withholding taxes, and registering the deed in the Public Registry.

Sources: National Notary College of Mexico & Ministry of Governance.

Unlike in the US or Canada, a notary in Mexico is an experienced lawyer appointed by the government with public authority. heir role is neutral and essential: hey are legally responsible for verifying the chain of title, ensuring that there are no encumbrances, calculating and withholding taxes, and registering the deed in the Public Registry.

Sources: National Notary College of Mexico & Ministry of Governance.
